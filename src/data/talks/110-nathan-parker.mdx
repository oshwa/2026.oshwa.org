When COVID-19 shattered supply chains, makers produced ~48M units of open-source medical supplies, but wound up spending the majority of their effort manually coordinating and match-making across ad hoc supply chains instead of actual production. This talk explores the open-source infrastructure we are building to solve this, in anticipation of the next crisis that affects our brittle supply chains: machine-readable, universal open-hardware data standards (OKH/OKW); automated matching engines; supply tree generation; and off-grid comms networks.
